Reduce Daily Resin Requirement — From 160 to 120 or 90

The current system forces players to spend 160 resin daily to efficiently progress — a time commitment that directly clashes with a player base that is growing older and busier. The top churn reason in this study is "No time to play" (22 mentions), and the most requested improvement is generous rewards (43 mentions). Both point to the same root cause: the reward-to-time ratio is too demanding. Reducing the daily resin cap to 120 or 90 would allow casual players to feel "complete" without investing 10 minutes daily — directly addressing the time pressure that is pushing players toward faster-paced competitors like Honkai: Star Rail.

📋 Survey: "No time" = #1 churn reason (22 mentions) 📋 Survey: Generous rewards = #1 desired improvement (43 mentions) 🌏 Community: Resin frustration = top negative keyword EN + ZH 🤖 SHAP: Event participation = #1 retention predictor

Introduce Free 10-Pull Per Version — Align with HoYoverse's Other Titles

Honkai: Star Rail and Zenless Zone Zero both provide a free 10-pull per version as standard — a policy Genshin Impact has not adopted. With churned players in this study citing spending pressure as a key departure reason, and active players requesting free pulls (38 mentions) as their second-most desired improvement, the absence of this feature is an increasingly visible disadvantage. As community discussion increasingly compares Genshin unfavourably against its own publisher's newer titles, not matching this baseline erodes perceived value even for players who are not spending money.

📋 Survey: Free pulls = #2 desired improvement (38 mentions) 📋 Survey: Spending pressure = churn factor 🌏 Community: Gacha cost = top negative cluster across all platforms ⚔️ Competitor: HSR + ZZZ both provide free 10-pull per version

Upgrade Anniversary Rewards — Free Limited 5★ Character, Weapon, or Monthly Card

Anniversary events are the single strongest re-engagement spike visible in the community heatmap — surpassing even new region launches. Yet the survey shows that churned players' #3 return condition is "generous anniversary rewards", implying that the current anniversary package is not compelling enough to bring them back. Upgrading the anniversary reward to include a free limited 5★ character, a limited 5★ signature weapon, or at minimum a free monthly card would transform the anniversary from a retention event into a genuine win-back opportunity — targeting all three churn archetypes simultaneously, especially the Watching & Waiting (36%) cohort who are still paying attention.

📋 Survey: Anniversary rewards = #3 return condition for churned players 📊 K-Means: Watching & Waiting archetype = 36% of churned — still reachable 🌏 Community: Anniversary = peak engagement spike on all 3 platforms

🟡 Medium Priority

Replace Patch-End Filler Events with Innovative, Replayable Content

The community heatmap clearly shows engagement dropping in the final weeks of every patch cycle when only rerun banners and repetitive daily commissions remain. The current response — Wondrous Creatures (千星奇遇) — has been received negatively by players who find it a shallow distraction rather than meaningful content. The recommendation is to invest in events that have genuine replayability and variety: limited co-op challenges, community-voted story fragments, or time-limited roguelike modes would better sustain the engagement curve from patch launch through to the next cycle. The goal is to eliminate the predictable activity cliff that currently signals to players that there is "nothing to do" — one of the top self-reported reasons for taking breaks.

🌏 Community Heatmap: Engagement cliff at every patch-end 📋 Survey: Exploration fatigue = #2 churn reason (16 mentions) 🤖 SHAP: Event participation = #1 retention predictor 👎 Player signal: Wondrous Creatures received negatively in community

Invest in Combat Design — Not Just Higher Numbers

Dashboard 2 of the Customer Profile analysis reveals that Combat is the lowest-rated dimension of Nod-Krai across all player tenure groups — a damning finding given that new characters are released every patch at considerable production cost. The core problem is one of design philosophy, not content volume: new characters add new damage numbers, but the underlying combat loop never changes — position, buff, burst, repeat. Veterans who have played across every region have run essentially the same Spiral Abyss rotation for years, and no amount of new character kits resolves that structural monotony.

This is ultimately a challenge for combat designers to address at a fundamental level — introducing mechanics that change how combat feels, not just what numbers appear on screen. Whether that takes the form of a roguelike endgame mode with dynamic modifiers, version-specific combat rulesets, or entirely new interaction systems, the direction needs to come from design intention rather than incremental character releases.

📊 Dashboard 2: Combat = lowest-rated Nod-Krai dimension 📋 Survey: Content repetition cited as churn factor 🌏 Community: Combat repetition cluster in Bilibili negative sentiment

Expand Co-op with Fun, Non-Combat Multiplayer Activities

SHAP analysis identifies social and community engagement as a retention factor, yet Genshin's co-op system remains one of the most underdeveloped features in a game of its scale. The key distinction here is intentional: the recommendation is not to add more combat co-op — combat fatigue is already identified as a pain point. Instead, the focus should be on genuinely fun, player-friendly multiplayer activities that bring people together without the friction of optimised team composition:

• Friendly competitive mini-games — fishing competitions, treasure-hunt races, instrument ensemble challenges, building contests
• Community exploration events — shared map objectives where groups of players collectively unlock rewards
• Social seasonal events — themed activities during festivals that naturally encourage playing with friends rather than requiring it

The goal is to make playing with others feel like a bonus rather than a chore, converting solitary sessions into social experiences that give players a reason to log in even when personal motivation dips.

🤖 SHAP: Social engagement = retention feature 📋 Survey: Community & friends = retention motivator ⚠️ Note: Avoid combat-heavy co-op — combat fatigue already identified

🟡 Medium Priority

Systematically Implement QoL Improvements — Starting Now, Every Other Version

A recurring theme across survey responses and community discussion is that players feel ignored — not just dissatisfied, but specifically that HoYoverse has not acted on feedback that has existed for years (artifact strongbox improvements, resin overflow, skip-able cutscenes, in-game friend activity, etc.). In an era where Honkai: Star Rail adapts to player feedback visibly and quickly, Genshin's slower response cycle is itself a churn signal.

The recommendation is concrete: starting from the very next version, dedicate every other patch to a QoL update — explicitly framed as "we heard you" — addressing the longest-standing player-requested improvements in sequence. This creates a visible, predictable commitment that players can point to as proof their feedback matters, directly rebuilding the trust that retains long-term players and differentiating Genshin from its own reputation for ignoring community input.

📋 Survey: Player-requested improvements repeatedly cited 🌏 Community: "Same issues never fixed" — recurring negative cluster ⚔️ Competitor: HSR QoL updates cited positively in comparison threads

Improve Story Accessibility — Skip Options & Alternative Narrative Formats

Survey data confirms that story and narrative investment is a primary retention driver — players stay because they are emotionally attached to characters and lore. Yet the current story delivery system has two friction points that undermine this asset:

① Add a proper skip/recap system. Players who have already completed quests, or who return after a long absence, should be able to skip or quickly recap story content without sitting through the full unskippable dialogue sequence. This is a basic accessibility feature that most modern narrative games include as standard.

② Offer richer alternative story formats for skipped content. Simply skipping leaves players with no context. A better approach — already demonstrated by HoYoverse themselves in Genshin's early days — would be to present skipped story as a short manga panel sequence (similar to Genshin's own early promotional comics) or a condensed CG compilation that conveys the emotional beats without requiring the full runtime. This preserves narrative investment for players who want to move fast, while rewarding those who engage fully with the complete version.

Together, these changes protect narrative as a retention asset while removing the friction that currently makes returning players feel overwhelmed by content backlogs.

📋 Survey: Story & character design = top retention motivators 📊 Dashboard 2: Veterans rate Story higher — narrative value confirmed 🌏 HoYolab: Lore & storyline = dominant positive discussion cluster 💡 Precedent: Genshin's own early manga format proves format viability
