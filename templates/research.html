Background

The Problem: Player Churn in F2P Games

Free-to-play (F2P) mobile games like Genshin Impact operate on a revenue model entirely dependent on player retention. With global mobile gaming revenue exceeding USD $92 billion in 2023, understanding why players disengage has become a critical business challenge.

🎮

Genshin Impact Context

Developed by HoYoverse, Genshin Impact is a live-service RPG with a global monthly active user base exceeding 60 million. Revenue is driven entirely by gacha (randomised item draws), making player retention directly linked to financial performance.

📉

The Churn Problem

Player churn — when an active player stops engaging with a game — is costly to reverse. Acquiring new players costs 5–7× more than retaining existing ones. Understanding churn signals early allows developers to intervene before players disengage permanently.

🔍

Gap in Existing Research

Most churn prediction studies rely on server-log data available only to game companies. Survey-based research capturing players' subjective motivations, social influences, and content preferences remains underexplored — especially for Genshin Impact specifically.

🧠

Explainability Gap

Even when prediction models are built, many are "black boxes" that tell developers who will churn but not why. This project uses SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to bridge that gap and make model outputs actionable.

Research Questions

What This Study Asks

Three core questions guide the entire research design and analytical pipeline.

RQ1 — Behavioural Patterns

What behavioural patterns and engagement characteristics distinguish active players who are at risk of churning from those who intend to continue playing Genshin Impact?

RQ2 — Predictive Features

Which in-game and demographic features are the most significant predictors of player retention and churn, as identified through machine learning models with SHAP explainability?

RQ3 — Segmentation & Community Signal

How do unsupervised clustering methods (K-Means, SOM) reveal distinct player segments among active and churned populations, and how do community sentiment signals across platforms corroborate the survey findings?

Study Objectives

Three Research Objectives

Mapped directly to the research questions, these objectives define the measurable deliverables of this project.

Objective 1

Discover Hidden Patterns

Automatically discover hidden behavioural patterns among active and churned player groups through unsupervised clustering (K-Means, SOM) and visualise distinct player segments within the survey dataset.

Objective 2

Predict & Segment

Build and evaluate supervised classification models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest) to predict player retention likelihood and churn probability, with SHAP explainability to identify the most influential behavioural drivers.

Objective 3

Evaluate with Standard Metrics

Assess model performance using standard evaluation metrics — AUC-ROC, precision, recall, F1-score — and contextualise results within the known limitations of the survey-scale dataset.
