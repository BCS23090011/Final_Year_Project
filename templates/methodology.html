Methodology

From survey design to machine learning —
a CRISP-DM guided analytical pipeline.

Framework

CRISP-DM Research Framework

This study adopts the Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining (CRISP-DM), a proven iterative framework that structures the research from problem understanding through to deployment. Each phase of the CRISP-DM cycle is mapped to a concrete deliverable in this project.

1

Business Understanding

Define the research problem: understanding player churn behaviour in Genshin Impact and building predictive models that can identify at-risk players before they disengage. Three research objectives (O1–O3) were formulated to guide the entire pipeline.

2

Data Understanding

Primary data collection via a structured online survey distributed to active Genshin Impact players (n=126) and churned players (n=44). Survey items covered demographics, in-game behaviour, motivations, social influence, content satisfaction, and spending behaviour. Community data was additionally scraped from 3 platforms (Google Play Store, YouTube, and Bilibili/HoYolab).

3

Data Preparation

Extensive feature engineering produced 171 features for the active player dataset and 83 for the churned player dataset. Multi-select survey responses were one-hot encoded. Ordinal Likert responses were encoded as ordered integers. A long-format file was created for multi-choice questions (Active_MultiSelect: 1,435 rows; Churn_MultiSelect: 459 rows).

4

Modelling

Four complementary modelling approaches were applied: (i) K-Means clustering (K=2) on active and churned player datasets; (ii) Self-Organising Map (SOM, 8×8 grid) for topological visualisation of active players; (iii) Logistic Regression and Random Forest classifiers for churn and retention prediction; (iv) SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) for model explainability and feature attribution.

5

Evaluation

Models evaluated using AUC-ROC, precision, recall, F1-score, and classification reports. The retention model achieved 0.97 overall accuracy (weighted F1: 0.96), though macro F1 of 0.79 reflects class imbalance. The churn prediction model: LR AUC = 0.708, RF AUC = 0.648. K-Means cluster validity confirmed via Elbow Method. SOM quality assessed via Quantization Error (QE=1.6229) and Topographic Error (TE=0.4206).

6

Deployment

Findings are presented through this interactive web portfolio — serving as the FYP prototype delivery platform. Results are contextualised with actionable recommendations for game developers on targeted re-engagement strategies and future data pipeline improvements.

Data Collection

Survey Design & Sampling

Two separate questionnaires were designed — one for active players and one for churned players — reflecting their different analytical roles in the study.

📋 Active Players Survey (n=126)

Focus: Predictive analysis — identifying who is at risk of churning.

  • Login frequency (7-day and 30-day)
  • Daily playtime & exploration progress
  • Event participation & task completion
  • Spiral Abyss engagement
  • Gacha spending behaviour
  • Nod-Krai content satisfaction (6 dimensions, 1–5 Likert)
  • Social influence (co-op, friend network)
  • Continuation intention (5-point scale)

📋 Churned Players Survey (n=44)

Focus: Descriptive analysis — understanding why players left and return conditions.

  • Reasons for stopping play (multi-select)
  • Whether still watching game previews
  • Migration to other HoYoverse games
  • Friend influence on return decision
  • Conditions that would encourage return
  • Reasons for permanent disengagement
  • Platform of play before churning

Important design note: The two surveys deliberately share only five demographic features (age, gender, play duration, friend influence). Behavioural variables such as login frequency, spending, and event participation were only collected from active players — a design limitation that constrained the combined churn model to demographic features only.

Machine Learning Models

Four Complementary Techniques

No single model is sufficient. This study combines unsupervised, supervised, and explainability approaches to answer the research questions from multiple angles.

🔵

K-Means Clustering

Applied separately to active (K=2) and churned (K=2) player datasets. K selected via the Elbow Method. PCA used for 2D visualisation. Produces behavioural segment profiles for targeted intervention design.


Unsupervised Objective 1
🟣

Self-Organising Map (SOM)

8×8 grid applied to the active player dataset (n=126) for topological neighbourhood-preserving visualisation. Complementary to K-Means — agreement rate between SOM and K-Means cluster assignments validates both methods.


Unsupervised Objective 1
🟢

Logistic Regression

Linear classifier applied to both churn prediction (Active vs Churned) and retention prediction (5-class ordinal). Acts as a baseline interpretable model. Notably outperformed Random Forest on the churn task (AUC LR=0.708 vs RF=0.648) — a pattern expected with low-dimensional feature spaces.


Supervised Objective 2 & 3
🟠

Random Forest + SHAP

Ensemble classifier providing non-linear modelling capability. SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) applied post-hoc to identify feature-level importance with directional attribution — revealing not just which features matter, but how they influence predictions.


Supervised Explainability Objective 2 & 3
Secondary Data Source

4-Platform Community Sentiment Analysis

To contextualise survey findings within the broader player community, public sentiment data was collected from three platforms and analysed using NLP and K-Means topic modelling.

Data Sources

  • Google Play Store — App ratings & review text in both English and Chinese (version-level score tracking)
  • YouTube — Comments from official HoYoverse patch preview videos and user-generated content
  • Bilibili / HoYolab — Comments from official HoYoverse uploads and user-generated content (Bilibili: Chinese-language; HoYolab: English-language)

Analysis Methods

  • K-Means on TF-IDF vectors — Topic clustering of community discussions
  • LLM post-hoc labelling — Cluster labels generated by LLM as interpretation layer
  • Engagement volume heatmap — Platform × version period cross-analysis
  • Negative keyword extraction — Google Play (EN + ZH) & Bilibili comment mining
  • Version-level score tracking — Correlating content patches with Google Play rating drops
