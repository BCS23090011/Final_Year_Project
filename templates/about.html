Project Background

Why Genshin Impact?

This FYP was motivated by a genuine interest in the intersection of game design, player psychology, and data science — areas where machine learning can deliver real business value.

Research Motivation

Genshin Impact represents one of the most interesting case studies in the modern gaming industry: a free-to-play game that generated over USD $4 billion in its first year through a purely voluntary spending model. Understanding why some players convert, some remain free-to-play, and some churn entirely has both academic and commercial significance.

As a computer science student, this project allowed me to apply the full ML pipeline — from survey design and feature engineering, through clustering and classification, to SHAP explainability — on a real-world problem with genuine complexity: imbalanced classes, multi-select responses, and the challenge of making black-box models interpretable to non-technical stakeholders.

Technical Skills Applied

This project exercised a broad range of data science competencies:

  • Survey instrument design — constructing valid, reliable questionnaire items for ML use
  • Feature engineering — one-hot encoding, ordinal encoding, multi-select flattening (171 features)
  • Unsupervised learning — K-Means, Self-Organising Maps, Elbow Method, PCA visualisation
  • Supervised learning — Logistic Regression, Random Forest, AUC-ROC evaluation
  • Explainability — SHAP beeswarm plots, directional attribution, feature importance ranking
  • NLP & community analytics — TF-IDF, K-Means topic modelling, LLM-assisted cluster labelling
  • Visualisation — Matplotlib/Seaborn for analysis, Tableau for customer profiling
  • Web development — Flask, HTML/CSS for this portfolio platform
FYP Delivery

Objective Completion

Objective 1 ✅

Hidden Pattern Discovery

Completed via K-Means (K=2) and SOM (8×8) clustering on both active and churned datasets. Per-cluster SHAP profiles produced for segment-specific insight.

Objective 2 ✅

Segmentation & Prediction

LR and RF classifiers built for both retention prediction (5-class) and churn prediction (binary). SHAP explainability applied with beeswarm plots and directional feature attribution.

Objective 3 ✅

Standard Evaluation Metrics

AUC-ROC (LR=0.708, RF=0.648), classification report (Accuracy=0.97, Macro F1=0.79), and class-level precision/recall/F1 documented with honest interpretation of class imbalance limitations.
