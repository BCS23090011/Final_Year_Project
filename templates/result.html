Objective 1
Unsupervised Learning

Player Segmentation via Clustering

K-Means and Self-Organising Maps were applied to discover natural groupings among active and churned players without using any labels.

K Selection (Elbow Method)

The Elbow Method was used to determine the optimal number of clusters. K=2 was selected for the active dataset, while K=1 was retained for the churned dataset due to the smaller sample size (n=44), representing the High Engagement vs Lower Engagement distinction.

Active Player K-Means (K=2)

PCA-reduced 2D visualisation of active player clusters. Cluster 0: Highly engaged, frequent login, high event participation. Cluster 1: Lower engagement, potential at-risk segment.

Churned Player Churn Reasons (K=1)

Due to the small churned sample (n=44), K resolved to 1 — producing a global churn reason prevalence profile rather than distinct sub-clusters.No Time to Play (59.1%) is the most prevalent reason, followed by Exploration Feels Tiring and Rewards Not Worth the Effort (both 47.7%).

Active Player SOM (8×8)

Self-Organising Map topology of active players. The SOM produced QE=1.6229 and TE=0.4206, above ideal thresholds — attributable to the small n=126 sample relative to the 64-node grid. Results are interpreted as exploratory topological visualisation.

K-Means vs SOM Agreement

Cross-validation of clustering results. The agreement rate between K-Means and SOM cluster assignments provides mutual validation, confirming that the two-segment structure is robust across methods.

Active Per-Cluster SHAP Profiles

SHAP feature importance broken down by K-Means cluster. This reveals which features drive retention risk within each segment — enabling cluster-specific intervention strategies for game developers.

The Balance — Why Stay vs Why Leave

Retention and churn are driven by different things entirely.
Green = what keeps players: character interest (r=0.53), login habit, event participation.
Red = why they leave: no time (59%), exploration fatigue (48%), rewards not worth it (48%).
Two separate problems — two separate fixes.

Objective 2
SHAP Explainability

Key Behavioural Drivers of Retention & Churn

SHAP values reveal not just which features matter, but in which direction and with what magnitude. These findings translate model outputs into actionable design recommendations.

Retention Feature Correlation

Correlation matrix of key behavioural features with the retention target. Event participation, login frequency, and task completion emerge as strongly correlated with retention intention — establishing the foundation for SHAP feature selection.

RF SHAP: Top 10 Retention Features

The 10 most important features for predicting retention. Interest in Upcoming Characters takes #1 (1.4432), with Nod-Krai Storyline Rating close behind at #2 (1.1659). Notably, Login Frequency (7-day) enters at #4 and Event Participation at #6 — confirming that active behavioural engagement sits alongside content interest as a core retention driver.

RF SHAP: Top 15 Retention Features

Extended view of the top 15 features. Interest in Upcoming Characters leads at #1, with Nod-Krai Storyline Rating at #2, Play Status: Active Weekly at #3, and Login Frequency (7-day) at #4 — a notable shift placing active play behaviour higher up. New entries include Fixed Friend Group and PlayStatus: Casual player, suggesting social context and play style also carry meaningful retention weight. Age, WatchMotivation, and Nod-Krai New Characters Rating drop off this list.

RF SHAP: Directional Attribution (Retention)

Signed SHAP values reveal a mixed directional pattern. Features such as Login Frequency (7-day & 30-day), Event Participation, Watches Game Previews, and Enjoys Story Quests positively increase retention probability (teal). In contrast, Interest in Upcoming Characters, Nod-Krai Storyline Rating, Play Status: Active Weekly, and Spending on Natlan Content show negative direction (red) — reflecting the ordinal target structure where high engagement on specific dimensions alone does not guarantee the highest retention class.

SHAP Beeswarm: Retention ⭐

The beeswarm plot reveals the full distribution of individual SHAP values across all 126 active players. Compared to earlier views, Event Participation, PlayStatus: Casual player, and Fixed Friend Group now appear — highlighting that both social ties and play frequency patterns contribute to individual-level retention risk. Players with high interest in upcoming characters and strong Nod-Krai story ratings cluster at positive (rightward) SHAP values. This is the study's most informative single chart.

SHAP Beeswarm: Churn Direction

SHAP beeswarm for the churn model. Feature distributions show that the combined churn model was constrained to 5 demographic features — explaining moderate AUC scores and the counterintuitive LR outperformance over RF.

Top Churn Reasons

Survey-reported reasons for stopping play. No time to play (22) leads, followed by exploration fatigue (16) and story not being attractive (13). Reveals churn is often situational, not permanent.

No-Return Reasons

Among the permanently churned archetype, reasons for never returning. Complete loss of interest and negative perceptions of the gacha monetisation system dominate — suggesting fundamentally different re-engagement barriers.

Encourage Return Conditions

What would bring churned players back? Generous rewards (18) and Free pulls (14) dominate — directly pointing to reward system improvements as the primary re-engagement lever.

Stop HoYoverse Reasons

Among players who stopped all HoYoverse games, reasons include gacha spending pressure and content fatigue. Wuthering Waves (Kuro Games) was identified as the primary competitor absorbing churned Genshin players.

Objective 3
Model Evaluation

Standard Performance Metrics

Models evaluated on AUC-ROC, precision, recall, and F1-score. Results must be interpreted in the context of severe class imbalance and small sample size.

Model Performance Summary (Churn AUC + Retention CV Accuracy)

Three metrics displayed: Churn model LR AUC = 0.708 and RF AUC = 0.648 (binary churn task), plus the Retention Model RF 5-Fold CV Accuracy = 0.746 (multi-class). LR outperforming RF on the churn task is consistent with RF's known underperformance in low-dimensional feature spaces (only 5 demographic features). Note these metrics are not directly comparable — AUC is binary, CV Accuracy is multi-class.

Retention Model — Classification Report (TEST Set, Out-of-Sample)

Test-set accuracy = 0.69 on the 20% hold-out (n=26). Only classes 2, 3, and 4 appear in the test split due to small class sizes for 0 and 1. Class 2 F1 = 0.00 — the model still cannot identify the higher-risk retention classes due to severe class imbalance. Class 4 (intends to continue) achieves F1 = 0.84. The 5-Fold CV accuracy of 0.746 provides a more reliable generalisation estimate than the single train/test split.

Bridge Analysis
Connecting the Two Groups

Active vs Churned — The Bridge Chart

The most unique contribution of this study: mapping churned player stated reasons to active player behavioural gradients — creating a predictive bridge between the two datasets.

Bridge Analysis: Churn Reasons → Active Player Risk Gradients ⭐

This chart is the methodological centrepiece of the study. By connecting what churned players cited as their reasons for leaving to the corresponding behavioural variables measured in the active player dataset, the model generates a risk gradient that can identify which currently-active players are exhibiting early-stage patterns matching known churn precursors. This is the primary tool for translating descriptive churn analysis into predictive warning signals.
